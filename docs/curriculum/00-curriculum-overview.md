# The AI Operator Curriculum

## Signal Forge Learning Program for Business Professionals

---

## Why This Curriculum Exists

### The Gap in AI Education

Most AI education falls into two camps:

1. **Technical courses** that teach Python, machine learning, and model architecture — useful for engineers, inaccessible to everyone else
2. **"AI for Everyone" courses** that explain concepts but don't build practical skills — you understand AI exists, but can't do anything with it

Neither prepares business professionals for the reality of 2026: **organizations need people who can operationalize AI** — not just use ChatGPT for writing emails, but design workflows, evaluate tools, manage AI projects, and translate business needs into AI solutions.

### The AI Operator Role

We call this role the **AI Operator** — someone who:

- Designs AI-augmented business processes
- Evaluates build vs. buy decisions
- Manages AI quality and risk
- Measures and communicates AI ROI
- Bridges the gap between technical teams and business stakeholders

This curriculum transforms you from **AI User** (someone who prompts ChatGPT) to **AI Operator** (someone who designs how AI works in an organization).

| Role specialization | Different AI "personas" for different tasks |

| Human-in-the-loop | Editor approval gates |
| Tool orchestration | Research agents, document readers, web search |
| Output formats | Same content → Word, PDF, PowerPoint, HTML |
| Mode switching | Different workflows for different content types |

## How This Curriculum Works

### The Why-What-How Framework

Every module follows a consistent structure:

**WHY** — The business problem this skill solves
- What goes wrong without this skill?
- What opportunities does this skill unlock?
- Real-world scenarios where this matters

**WHAT** — The concepts and mental models
- Key vocabulary and definitions
- Frameworks for thinking about the problem
- How Signal Forge implements these concepts

**HOW** — Hands-on practice
- Business Lab exercises (practical scenarios)
- Deliverables you'll create
- Assessment criteria

### Progressive Skill Building

```
Phase 1: AI Literacy          → You understand what AI can/can't do
    ↓
Phase 2: Workflow Engineering → You can design AI-ready processes
    ↓
Phase 3: Implementation       → You can build working solutions
    ↓
Phase 4: Strategy & Economics → You can make AI investment decisions
```

Each phase builds on the previous. You can't design good workflows without understanding AI constraints. You can't evaluate ROI without understanding implementation costs.

### Business Labs

Every module includes **Business Labs** — practical exercises that simulate real internship scenarios:

- **Lab 1-2**: Prompt engineering and AI communication
- **Lab 3-4**: Process documentation and data structuring
- **Lab 5**: No-code automation building
- **Lab 6-7**: Strategic analysis and recommendations

Labs produce **deliverables** you can add to your portfolio — not just completed exercises, but artifacts that demonstrate competency to future employers.

## Curriculum Structure

### Phase 1: AI Literacy & Mechanics (Weeks 1-2)

**The physics of AI — what it can and can't do**

| Module | Topic | Key Concepts |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| 1.1 | The Economics of Intelligence | Tokens, inference costs, when AI is worth it |
| 1.2 | Context and Memory | Context windows, RAG, what AI "remembers" |
| 1.3 | Providers and Models | OpenAI vs. Anthropic vs. Google — when to use what |
| 1.4 | Prompting as Management | Chain-of-thought, personas, structured output |

**Signal Forge Connection**: Understanding the `providers/` directory — why multiple AI providers exist and when each is used.

**Business Labs**: Persona Stress Test, Chain of Thought Audit

---

### Phase 2: Workflow Engineering (Weeks 3-4)

**Designing processes AI can execute**

| Module | Topic | Key Concepts |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| 2.1 | Decomposition | Breaking vague goals into atomic tasks |
| 2.2 | Role-Based Workflows | Specialization, handoffs, quality gates |
| 2.3 | Human-in-the-Loop Design | Where humans add value, where AI scales |
| 2.4 | Quality and Iteration | Feedback loops, scoring, thresholds |

**Signal Forge Connection**: The `roles/` directory and the Ghost Writer → Copywriter → Editor pipeline. Understanding why each role exists and what it optimizes for.

**Business Labs**: SOP Translation, Unstructured Data Cleanup

---

### Phase 3: No-Code Implementation (Weeks 5-6)

**Building without coding**

| Module | Topic | Key Concepts |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| 3.1 | Triggers and Actions | Event-driven automation fundamentals |
| 3.2 | Tool Integration | Connecting AI to real business systems |
| 3.3 | Data Flow Design | Inputs, transformations, outputs |
| 3.4 | Error Handling | What happens when AI fails |

**Signal Forge Connection**: The `tools/` directory (web search, document reader) and `exporters/` (how content flows to different output formats).

**Business Labs**: Zapier Hello World, Multi-Format Export Challenge

---

### Phase 4: AI Strategy & Economics (Weeks 7-8)

**Making decisions that create value**

| Module | Topic | Key Concepts |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| 4.1 | Build vs. Buy Analysis | When to use off-the-shelf vs. custom |
| 4.2 | AI ROI Calculation | Costs, benefits, payback period |
| 4.3 | Risk and Governance | Hallucinations, compliance, guardrails |
| 4.4 | Change Management | Getting organizations to adopt AI |

**Signal Forge Connection**: The `voice/` validation system (quality guardrails), mode selection logic (matching tools to tasks), and iteration controllers (knowing when "good enough" is reached).

**Business Labs**: Buy vs. Build Memo, Agentic Research Report

### Competency Demonstrations

| Phase | Deliverable | What It Proves |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | Prompt Library | You can communicate effectively with AI |
| 2 | Workflow Design Document | You can architect AI-ready processes |

### The Capstone Project

The curriculum concludes with a **Shadow Project**:

1. Identify a real process (internship, student org, part-time job)
2. Document the current state (human workflow)
3. Design the AI-augmented future state
4. Build a working prototype (no-code)
5. Calculate ROI and write recommendation memo

This becomes the centerpiece of your AI Operator portfolio.
